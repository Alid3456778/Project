Statue Of Liberty

About :

A symbol of freedom and democracy, the Statue of Liberty welcomes all to New York Harbor. Gifted by France, Lady Liberty stands tall, holding a torch and a tablet inscribed with the date of American independence. Visit this iconic monument to experience the spirit of liberty and the enduring values it represents.

Images :

The Statue of Liberty was a gift from the people of France to the United States, dedicated on October 28, 1886, as a symbol of friendship and freedom.

Designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and its internal structure engineered by Gustave Eiffel, the statue stands at 151 feet (46 meters) and is made of copper, with its distinctive green color resulting from oxidation.

The statue, holding a torch and a tabula ansata (a tablet evoking the law), represents freedom and democracy. Its location on Liberty Island in New York Harbor makes it a prominent symbol welcoming immigrants arriving by sea to the United States.

Location:

The Statue of Liberty is located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, New York City, USA.

Symbolism:

1 .Liberty and Freedom:

The Statue of Liberty is a symbol of freedom, democracy, and the enduring values of the United States. It is often referred to as "Lady Liberty."

2 .New York Harbor Entrance:

The statue was strategically placed at the entrance of New York Harbor, welcoming immigrants arriving by sea and serving as a symbol of hope and opportunity.

3 .Colossus of Rhodes Inspiration:

he statue's design was inspired in part by the ancient Colossus of Rhodes, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

Best Time to Visit/Events and Celebrations::

The Statue of Liberty is often featured in various events and celebrations, including the Independence Day celebrations in the United States.

Access:

1 .Visitor Access:

Visitors can access Liberty Island and the statue by taking a ferry from Battery Park in Manhattan.

2 .Crown Access:

Access to the crown is limited and requires a separate reservation due to its popularity and limited capacity.
